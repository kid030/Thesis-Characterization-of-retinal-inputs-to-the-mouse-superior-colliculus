Visual response properties of the superior colliculus

Dräger and Hubel were the first to report the distinct visual response properties of individual neurons in the superficial layers of the mouse SC (Dräger and Hubel, 1975a,b). They found SC neurons respond best to a small, slowly moving object of any shape within the receptive-field and a subset of SC neurons are direction-selective to the moving stimuli. In later research, Wang et al (2010) conducted a quantitative analysis of visual response properties using single-unit extracellular recordings from superficial layers of the SC in anesthetized mice. They found that most SC neurons respond to both onset and offset of the flashing spot with the preferred radius of 6-10°, similar to their receptive field size. Besides, they also detailed how individual SC neurons are tuned to direction and orientation selectivity, spatial and temporal frequency, and response linerity. Around 30% of cells displayed high direction-selective responses (DSI≥0.5) and ~20% of cells displayed high orientation-selective responses (OSI≥0.5, DSI<0.5). It is also noted that the direction-selectivity has been observed across several species including primates (Albano et al. 1978, Cynader & Berman 1972, Fortin et al. 1999, Masland et al. 1971, McIlwain & Buser 1968, Michael 1972, Rhoades & Chalupa 1976), which conservation is suggestive of a specialized function of the SC in visual processing. A recent report from de Malmazet et al (2018) using two-photon calcium imaging showed a retinotopic separation of both nasally and temporally tuned direction-selective neurons in the mouse SC. This separation resembles an optic flow field map, which indicated an important role of SC in guiding orientating or avoidance behavioral responses. 

Cell types of the mouse superior colliculus

More than 40 different cell types of RGCs have been identified based on their physical properties (Baden et al 2016). Most RGCs project to SC and each SC neurons is estimated to receive input from an average of 6 ganglion cells(Chandrasekaran et al 2017). Given the above facts, the potential number of distinct types of visual responses of SC neurons could be quite large, which makes it difficult to classify the SC neurons with their response properties. Traditionally SC neurons have been classified into multiple types based on their morphology in many species (May 2006). In the mouse, Gale and Murphy (2014 & 2018)  by characterizng the morphological and electrophysiological properties, identified several cell types. Wide-field cells, narrow-field cells and stellate cells are the excitatory cell types in the SC. Horizontal cells, which includes both interneurons and projection neurons are the inhibitory cell type in the SC. These cell types could be labeled with different transgenetc mouse line.
